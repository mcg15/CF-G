All Grown Up

Here are some herbs you can grow at home!

Basil

Basil likes lots of sunlight and water. Keep on a sunny windowsill where the plant can get around 6 hours of sunlight and water your basil plant everyday. Basil is delicious in pesto or as a garnish on pasta - just clip off the leaves as and when you need!

Parsley

Parsely also likes lots of sunlight - around 6-8 hours per day. You do't have to water parsely every day but make sure the soil stays damp to the touch. Parsely goes great in sauce to accompany fish.

Rosemary

Rosemary prefers drier soil so you don't need to water it as much. Rosemary is a low-maintenance kind of gal and doesn't need too much sun either, prefering a bright but cool spot. Rosemary is delicious with lamb.

Mint

Mint is an early riser and likes morning sun but shady afternoons. Mint also likes lots of water, so water ever 1-2 days. Mint makes great tea and is also an excellent accompanyment to fruit salad.

Chives

Chives like full sun so plant somewhere where it can catch lots of rays! It's important to keep chives well-watered so water every 2-3 days. Add chives so salads for a ligth onion-y kick!

Thyme

Thyme is super hard and doesn't need much watering at all. Water thyme - well whenever you have time! Let the soild become slightly dry between waterings. Thyme also likes lots of sun, but doesn't need direct sunny so can be grown anywhere as long as its bright. Thyme and honey on baked camembert is second to none.
